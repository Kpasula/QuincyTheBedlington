A Bedlington Terrier is the dog people stop you to ask about. The most common
first reaction is that it looks like **a little lamb** — and that's exactly the
point of the breed's distinctive shape and coat.

## The look

- **Lamb-like silhouette** — arched back, narrow build, a distinctive head shape with
  a topknot.
- **A curly, wool-like coat** that grows continuously (this is the single biggest
  practical fact about the breed — see [grooming](/grooming)).
- Coat colors include shades like **blue/gray/silver**, and the coat shape is
  maintained by regular breed-style trimming, not by shedding.

## Temperament

Owners are drawn to Bedlingtons for a specific mix:

- **Low shedding** and a unique, classy look.
- **Playful but not too much** — good for home life and companionship.
- A manageable **size** that suits a lot of living situations.

Like any terrier they have personality and energy, and puppies go through the usual
"bite everything, ignore all authority" phase — which is a stage, not a permanent trait.

## What living with one is really like

The two things that shape day-to-day life with a Bedlington the most:

1. **The coat needs ongoing grooming.** Low-shedding does not mean low-maintenance —
   it's the trade. See [how often to groom a Bedlington](/grooming/how-often-to-groom-a-bedlington).
2. **The breed has a known genetic health topic** — copper toxicosis — which is worth
   understanding early. See
   [copper toxicosis in Bedlington Terriers](/health/copper-toxicosis-in-bedlington-terriers).

## The short version

A Bedlington Terrier is a distinctive, lamb-like, low-shedding companion breed with
a coat that needs regular grooming and one well-known inherited health issue to be
aware of. Get those two things right and you've got a genuinely special dog — the
kind people cross the street to meet.
